## Overview

The Global Airbnb Performance Dashboard is a data analysis project built in POWER BI that provides a consolidated view of Airbnb’s worldwide listings, reviews, ratings, hosts, market share, customer behavior and seasonality. It is designed to support strategic decision‑making for leadership, sales, and marketing teams by transforming raw marketplace data into actionable insights.

## Growth & Supply Trends
The dashboard traces Airbnb’s evolution across phases of introduction, growth, maturity, decline, and reinvention. As noted in the analysis, “In 2015, Airbnb reached the highest number of new listings… 2016 and 2017 saw a restraint also due to the tightening of local regulations.” Despite regulatory challenges, Airbnb became profitable in late 2016 and sustained profitability through 2017. Growth resumed in 2018 but was disrupted by the COVID‑19 pandemic.

## Market Share by City
Paris, New York, and Sydney emerge as dominant markets, accounting for nearly half of total listings and 59% of reviews. Paris leads globally, supported by strong price competitiveness —“prices for hotel rooms being twice as much as Airbnb.” The dashboard visualizes this gap across hotel rooms, entire places, shared rooms, and private rooms.

## Ratings & Customer Sentiment
The ratings module highlights consistently high satisfaction across cities. Mexico City and Rio de Janeiro score highest overall, while Hong Kong and Istanbul rank lowest. Cleanliness and value show the weakest performance, whereas communication is the strongest metric across markets.

## Review Behaviour & Seasonality
Customer review frequency shows that 98.8% of reviewers post three reviews or fewer, indicating a predominance of first‑time or infrequent reviewers. Seasonality trends reveal strong summer peaks in Paris and Rome and holiday‑driven surges in New York.
## Trust & Verification
Trust metrics show a healthy ecosystem, with “over two-thirds of Airbnb hosts fully verified,” ensuring reliability and safety for guests.
## Concluding Insights
The dashboard reveals clear opportunities for strategic growth: strengthening performance in lower‑rated cities, leveraging seasonal demand peaks with targeted campaigns, and expanding host verification to reinforce trust. The combination of supply trends, customer sentiment, and market competitiveness provides a strong foundation for data‑driven decisions across product, marketing, and marketplace operations.
